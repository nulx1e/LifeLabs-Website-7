Typhoon Safety Tips

Before the Typhoon

Before a typhoon hits, it's important to take the following steps to ensure your safety:

  • Stay informed by listening to weather reports and following authorities' instructions.
  • Prepare an emergency kit that includes food, water, and other essentials that can last for several days.
  • Avoid typhoon-prone areas, such as low-lying areas, beaches, riverfronts, and areas with flooding.
  • Secure personal belongings and important documents in waterproof containers.

During the Typhoon

During a typhoon, it's crucial to stay indoors and avoid going outside. Here are some additional safety tips:

  • Turn off electronics and unplug them to avoid damage from power surges.
  • Listen to weather reports and follow authorities' instructions.
  • Check on your neighbors, especially those who may need assistance or are living alone.
  • Avoid personal watercraft, swimming, and other activities that could pose danger.

After the Typhoon

After a typhoon, it's important to assess property damage and take necessary steps to ensure safety. Here are some additional safety tips:

  • Callprofessionals if necessary to repair any damage.
  • Be aware of downed power lines and other hazards.
  • Do not touch or move downed power lines.
  • Stay away from flooded areas and do not attempt to drive through them.
